PFN Approximation of the PPD

Four factors govern how well \(q_{\widehat\theta}\) approximates the family of PPDs \(\{\pi(\cdot \mid \cdot,\, D_n)\}_{n}\):

  • Model capacity \(\{q_\theta : \theta \in \Theta\}\) must be expressive enough to represent \(\pi\);
  • Hypothesis space \(\Phi\) should be complex enough;
  • Monte-Carlo size: \(\widehat\theta\) \(\rightarrow\) \(\theta^*\) at rate \(\sim m^{-1/2}\) \(\Rightarrow\) a large number of steps \(m\) is needed;
  • Prior over context sizes ▶ view \(\mathbb{P}_N\) as PPD's complexity regularizer.
Mechanism: sampling N_j ~ P_N during pre-training means theta-hat averages theta*_{N_j} over many context sizes. At n = 1 the PPD is close to the prior average (nearly constant); as n grows, hypotheses phi incompatible with the data are filtered out and the PPD's functional complexity increases. So P_N acts as a direct regularizer on functional complexity. Curiously, TabPFN was trained only on N in {1, ..., 1023} yet still generalizes well to much larger n at test time -- the next section looks at why.

Bias-Variance Decomposition of PFNs

Beyond pre-training sizes the Bayesian view says nothing about \(q_{\widehat\theta}\). Frequentist view instead: treat \(q_{\widehat\theta}(y \mid x,\, \cdot)\) as an untrained predictor \(D_n \mapsto q_{\widehat\theta}(y \mid x, D_n)\), with tuning parameters \(\widehat\theta\) fixed by meta-learning.

Its estimation error under \(\mathcal{D}_n \sim p_0^n\) decomposes:

\[\begin{aligned} q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n) - p_0(y \mid x) \;=\;& \textcolor{teal}{\underbrace{\textcolor{black}{q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n) - \mathbb{E}_{\mathcal{D}_n \sim p_0^n}\big[q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n)\big]}}_{\textstyle\text{variance}}} \\[0.2em] &+\; \textcolor{blue}{\underbrace{\textcolor{black}{\mathbb{E}_{\mathcal{D}_n \sim p_0^n}\big[q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n)\big] - p_0(y \mid x)}}_{\textstyle\text{bias}}} \end{aligned}\]
Empirically this error keeps decreasing with \(n\) — even for \(n\) never seen in pre-training. Which structural features of \(q_\theta\) explain that?
The variance term: scatter of the prediction around its average over training sets drawn from p_0. The bias term: offset of that average from the truth. The Bayesian motivation (PPD approximation) only covers sample sizes from P_N; for larger n we need a different, frequentist explanation — the next three slides supply it: variance dies by architecture (diminishing sensitivity), bias only dies with locality.

Variance and Diminishing Sensitivity

What kills the variance? In larger contexts, a single sample should influence the prediction less. Suppose there are \(\alpha > 0\), \(L < \infty\) such that for large \(n\) and almost all \(D_n,\, D_n'\) differing in one sample,

\[ \big|\, q_{\theta}(y \mid x, D_n) - q_{\theta}(y \mid x, D_n') \,\big| \;\le\; L\, n^{-\alpha}. \tag{5} \]
Theorem 5.2 [4]
If (5) holds, then with high probability \[ \big|\, q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n) - \mathbb{E}\big[q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n)\big] \,\big| \;\lesssim\; n^{1/2 - \alpha}. \]
Lemma 5.3 [4]
If (5) holds with \(\alpha > 1/2\), then \[ q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n) - \mathbb{E}\big[q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n)\big] \xrightarrow{\;n \to \infty\;} 0 \quad \text{almost surely}. \]

Holds for every \(n\) — no pre-training on that size needed. ▶ swap one sample, watch the prediction (not) move

Read (5) as a bounded-differences / stability condition: replacing one sample moves the output by at most L n^-alpha. Theorem 5.2 is McDiarmid-style concentration: stability of order n^-alpha gives deviations of order n^(1/2-alpha). For alpha > 1/2 (Lemma 5.3) the deviation vanishes almost surely. Crucially this is a property of the ARCHITECTURE as a function of D_n, valid at any n — this is the part of the error that keeps improving on context sizes the network never saw. Remaining error: the bias (next slide).

Bias and the Need for Locality

The variance vanishes on its own; the bias is what remains. When can \(\mathbb{E}\big[q_\theta(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n)\big] \to p_0(y \mid x)\)? A necessary condition: the predictor must be local.

Theorem 5.4 [4]
Let \(\mathcal{P}\) be a set of distributions with \(\mathbb{E}_{\mathcal{D}_n \sim p^n}\big[q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n)\big] \xrightarrow{\;n \to \infty\;} p(y \mid x)\) for every \(p \in \mathcal{P}\) (vanishing bias). If (5) holds, there is \(\epsilon_n \to 0\) such that for every \(\tilde p \in \mathcal{P}\), almost surely \[ \big|\, q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n) - q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \widetilde{\mathcal{D}}_n) \,\big| \xrightarrow{\;n \to \infty\;} 0, \] where \(\widetilde{\mathcal{D}}_n\) keeps the features but resamples far-away labels: \[ Y^{(i)\prime} \begin{cases} = Y^{(i)}, & \text{if } \|X^{(i)} - x\| \le \epsilon_n, \\ \sim \tilde p(\cdot \mid X^{(i)}), & \text{if } \|X^{(i)} - x\| > \epsilon_n. \end{cases} \]

Unbiased on a rich \(\mathcal{P}\) \(\Rightarrow\) labels far from \(x\) can be scrambled arbitrarily without changing the prediction — only samples near \(x\) may matter. ▶ scramble far labels: local vs. global predictor

Intuition: if the predictor is to be asymptotically unbiased for EVERY plausible truth p, it cannot rely on far-away samples — under a different truth p-tilde those labels would be different, yet the predictor must converge to the right answer at x either way. So asymptotically only an epsilon_n-neighbourhood of x may contribute. Caveats (paper): this is only a necessary condition (the constant predictor is local but biased), and locality means the effective sample count is o(n) — a bias-variance trade-off: you cannot have (5) with alpha = 1 AND vanishing bias.

Transformer Networks

One-layer transformer: context \(V_i = (Y^{(i)}, X^{(i)})\), query \(v = (0, x)\). Attention head \(h\) weighs the samples by

\[ a_j^{(h)} = \operatorname{softmax}\big( v^\top W_q^{(h)} V_1, \dots, v^\top W_q^{(h)} V_n \big)_j, \qquad \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_j^{(h)} = 1. \]
Theorem 6.2 [4]
For bounded features \(\|x\| \le K\) and bounded weight matrices \(\|W_q^{(h)}\|, \|W_v^{(h)}\|, \|W_{r,1}\|, \|W_{r,2}\|, \|W_o\| < \infty\), \[ \big|\, q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n) - \mathbb{E}\big[q_{\theta}(y \mid x, \mathcal{D}_n)\big] \,\big| \;\lesssim\; n^{-1/2} \quad \text{with high probability}. \]
  • softmax normalization \(\Rightarrow\) each sample's weight is \(\sim 1/n\) \(\Rightarrow\) diminishing sensitivity (5) with \(\alpha = 1\);
  • so the variance vanishes at rate \(1/n\) for any \(\theta\) — it is the architecture, not the training. ▶ watch attention weights flatten as \(n\) grows
The attention weights of each head sum to one over the n context samples, so swapping one sample can move the attention summary by at most ~1/n — condition (5) with alpha = 1, and Theorem 5.2 turns that into the n^(-1/2) deviation bound. This holds irrespective of the tuned parameters theta-hat. The bias (Theorem 6.3) is the opposite: it depends entirely on theta, converges to a limit given by an exponentially tilted version of p_0 — attention localizes "softly" but never fully, so the bias does NOT vanish.

Discussion

Main insight
Transformers approximate the PPD — and keep learning beyond it.
  • with enough model capacity, a rich enough \(\Phi\) and enough Monte-Carlo steps, pre-training makes \(q_{\widehat\theta} \approx \pi\) — for context sizes from \(\mathbb{P}_N\);
  • the fourth ingredient, \(\mathbb{P}_N\), is necessarily limited (TabPFN: \(N \le 1023\)) — yet predictions keep improving on larger \(n\);
  • reason: attention forces the variance to vanish (\(\sim 1/n\)) for any \(\widehat\theta\) — generalization to unseen context sizes comes from the architecture;
  • the bias does not vanish — localization (e.g. nearest-neighbour contexts) is the remaining lever.
Wrap-up of the section's story: PPDs can learn (Thm 3.1); PFNs approximate PPDs when the four factors are right (S4); beyond pre-trained context sizes the Bayesian story ends, but the frequentist decomposition shows the variance dies by architecture (Thms 5.2/6.2), and the bias needs locality (Thm 5.4) — confirmed numerically by localized TabPFN.
