Conclusions
  • A single transformer, pre-trained once on millions of synthetic datasets, approximates the PPD and predicts on a new dataset in one forward pass — no per-dataset training or tuning.
  • Beats the strongest AutoML baselines on datasets up to \(10{,}000\) samples / \(500\) features, in seconds rather than hours.
Limitations
  • Focuses on datasets with up to 1 000 training samples, 100 purely numerical features without missing values and 10 classes; beyond these limits CatBoost / XGBoost / AutoGluon still win.
  • Memory scales quadratically with training dataset size, making inference hard on large datasets.
  • Supports only classification, without regression.
  • Only approximates causal reasoning over a distribution of SCMs, without explicitly performing it.
  • Only IID samples.
