\begin{frame}{TabPFN}
  \footnotesize
  \begin{block}{Conclusions}
    \begin{itemize}[<+->]
      \item A single transformer, pre-trained once on millions of synthetic datasets,
            approximates the PPD and predicts on a new dataset in one forward pass
            --- no per-dataset training or tuning.
      \item Beats the strongest AutoML baselines on datasets up to
            $10{,}000$ samples / $500$ features, in seconds rather than hours.
    \end{itemize}
  \end{block}
  \begin{alertblock}{Limitations}
    \begin{itemize}[<+->]
      \item Focuses on datasets with up to 1 000 training samples, 100 purely numerical
            features without missing values and 10 classes; beyond these limits
            CatBoost / XGBoost / AutoGluon still win.
      \item Memory scales \emph{quadratically} with training dataset size, making inference
            hard on large datasets.
      \item Supports only classification, without regression.
      \item Only approximates causal reasoning over a distribution of SCMs,
            without explicitly performing it.
    \end{itemize}
  \end{alertblock}
\end{frame}
