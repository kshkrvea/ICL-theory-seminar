Conclusion

Takeaways
  • Tabular Foundation Models amortize Bayesian inference by learning the family of PPDs.
  • Generalisation to context sizes never seen in training is an architectural property of the transformer.
  • Tabular Foundation Models are still a developing area of research, but they already:
    • Make predictions for a new dataset in a single forward pass, in seconds;
    • Achieve state of the art on small-to-medium tables.
Limitations
  • Current SOTA models are limited to mid-size datasets: up to 1M rows × 200 features, 100k × 2,000, or 1k × 20,000.
  • The paradigm is still i.i.d. prediction — no causal inference, time series, or other correlated data.
  • In some domains, prior misspecification may occur.
  • No support for multimodal data.
